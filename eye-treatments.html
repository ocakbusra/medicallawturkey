Understanding Eye Treatments in Türkiye

Eye treatments and eyelid surgeries, such as blepharoplasty, are popular cosmetic procedures sought by international patients in Türkiye. These treatments aim to improve the appearance of the eyelids, reduce puffiness, or correct drooping eyelids for a more youthful and refreshed look.

Some patients later report outcomes that differ from their expectations or pre-operative discussions.

Commonly Reported Issues After Eye Treatments

  • Asymmetry between the eyes or eyelids.
  • Over-correction or under-correction, with eyes appearing too hollow or too tight.
  • Visible scarring or unnatural eyelid folds.
  • Dry eyes, irritation, or difficulty closing the eyes completely.
  • Changes in eyelid position, including ectropion or entropion.
  • Persistent swelling or bruising beyond the expected healing period.
  • Results that look significantly different from the desired natural appearance.
  • Need for revision or corrective surgery.

These are general observations based on patient experiences shared worldwide. Not every unsatisfactory aesthetic result raises legal questions. In cosmetic procedures, the expected outcome and the quality of patient information are often important considerations under Turkish law.

Legal Framework for Aesthetic Procedures

Under Turkish law, aesthetic eye treatments and eyelid surgeries are generally evaluated within the scope of contractual obligations (eser sozlesmesi). When concerns arise, the focus is usually on whether the patient was adequately informed about possible results, risks and limitations, and whether the achieved outcome aligns with the discussed expectations.

The Importance of Informed Consent

In cosmetic surgery around the eye area, the duty to inform the patient is particularly important due to the sensitivity of the region. Patients are expected to receive clear information regarding:

  • The anticipated shape and openness of the eyes.
  • Possible risks such as dry eyes, asymmetry, or changes in eyelid function.
  • Healing process and realistic recovery timeline.
  • Available alternatives and potential need for revision.

Information for International Patients

Many patients return to their home countries shortly after eye treatments in Türkiye. Foreign patients generally have similar rights to Turkish citizens in matters related to medical liability. Initial steps in understanding a situation can often be handled remotely through proper documentation and communication.

Commonly Considered Steps

  • Collecting medical records, before-and-after photographs, consent forms and all correspondence with the clinic, including WhatsApp messages.
  • Obtaining an independent medical opinion where necessary.
  • Reviewing the general legal framework that may apply under Turkish law.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions Often Raised by Patients After Eye Treatment Procedures

General informational answers based on common patient concerns.

How closely should the final eyelid lift result align with what was discussed before surgery?

In eyelid lift (blepharoplasty) procedures, pre-operative discussions often focus on eyelid shape, openness of the eyes, symmetry, and the overall balance of the eye area. When specific outcomes are described or visually illustrated before surgery, the final result is generally considered in relation to those discussions.

From a general legal perspective, attention is often given to whether the achieved appearance reasonably corresponds with what was explained beforehand, taking into account anatomical factors and the inherent limitations of eyelid procedures.

What level of information is expected regarding functional changes and eye comfort?

Eyelid procedures involve an area closely related to eye function and comfort. In cosmetic eye surgery, patients are generally expected to receive clear and detailed information regarding:

  • Possible effects on eyelid closure and eye dryness.
  • Temporary or lasting irritation or sensitivity.
  • Limitations in achieving perfect symmetry.
  • Variations that may occur during the healing process.

General evaluations often focus on whether these possibilities were adequately explained before surgery, allowing patients to form realistic expectations.

How are concerns such as asymmetry or changes in eyelid position evaluated?

Following eyelid lift procedures, patients may notice asymmetry or changes in eyelid position during healing. In general terms, evaluations often consider whether such outcomes were discussed prior to surgery and how post-operative expectations were communicated.

The assessment typically depends on the content and clarity of pre-operative communications rather than on subjective dissatisfaction alone.

Does returning to another country affect the understanding of eyelid lift-related concerns?

Returning abroad after an eyelid lift procedure does not, by itself, prevent a patient from understanding their general position. Many relevant aspects involve reviewing medical records, consent documents, and pre-operative communications that remain accessible regardless of location.

As with other cosmetic procedures, general evaluations tend to focus on documentation and prior discussions rather than on physical presence.
