Understanding Dental Treatments Malpractice in Turkey

Dental treatments such as implants, veneers, crowns, smile makeovers and full-mouth restorations are among the most popular procedures sought by international patients in Türkiye. Many choose Turkey due to high-quality clinics and more affordable prices.

Some patients later report outcomes that differ from their expectations or pre-operative plans.

Commonly Reported Issues After Dental Treatments

  • Implants that fail to integrate or become loose.
  • Poorly fitting crowns, bridges or veneers.
  • Significant difference between the planned smile design and the final result.
  • Persistent pain, infection or gum problems.
  • Nerve damage or loss of sensation in the lips, tongue or gums.
  • Unnatural appearance or color mismatch.
  • Need for repeated corrective treatments or revision work.
  • Insufficient or unclear explanation of risks and long-term outcomes before treatment.

These are general observations based on patient experiences shared worldwide. Not every unsatisfactory dental result raises legal questions. In dental procedures, the expected outcome and the quality of patient information are often important considerations under Turkish law.

Legal Framework for Dental Procedures

Under Turkish law, dental treatments are generally evaluated within the scope of contractual obligations (eser sozlesmesi) and duty of care. When concerns arise, the focus is usually on whether the patient was adequately informed about possible results, risks, success rates and long-term expectations.

The Importance of Informed Consent

In dental treatments, especially cosmetic and implant procedures, the duty to inform the patient is significant. Patients are expected to receive clear information regarding:

  • The anticipated aesthetic and functional outcome.
  • Possible complications and success or failure rates.
  • Risks specific to implants, bone grafting or extensive restorations.
  • Alternatives and realistic long-term expectations.

Information for International Patients

Many patients return to their home countries shortly after dental treatment in Turkey. Foreign patients generally have similar rights to Turkish citizens in matters related to medical and dental liability. Initial steps in understanding a situation can often be handled remotely through proper documentation and communication.

Commonly Considered Steps

  • Collecting all treatment plans, before-and-after photographs, X-rays, consent forms and correspondence with the clinic, including WhatsApp messages.
  • Obtaining an independent dental opinion in your home country where necessary.
  • Reviewing the general legal framework that may apply under Turkish law.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions Often Raised by Patients After Dental Treatments

General informational answers based on common patient concerns.

How closely should the final dental result align with what was discussed before treatment?

In dental treatments, pre-treatment discussions often include explanations regarding tooth appearance, alignment, color, bite function, and overall oral aesthetics. When specific outcomes are described, illustrated, or digitally simulated prior to treatment, the final result is generally considered in relation to those discussions.

From a general legal perspective, attention is often given to whether the achieved result reasonably corresponds with what was communicated beforehand, taking into account the nature of the dental procedure and the information provided to the patient.

What level of information is expected regarding dental materials and treatment limitations?

Dental treatments may involve the use of various materials and techniques, such as implants, crowns, veneers, or bridges. In cosmetic and restorative dentistry, patients are generally expected to receive clear and detailed information regarding:

  • The type and characteristics of materials used.
  • Possible limitations affecting durability, appearance, or function.
  • The need for maintenance, follow-up, or future interventions.
  • Factors that may influence long-term outcomes.

General evaluations often focus on whether this information was communicated in a manner that allowed the patient to make an informed decision.

How are issues such as discomfort, bite problems, or aesthetic dissatisfaction evaluated?

Following dental treatments, patients may experience issues such as prolonged discomfort, bite misalignment, sensitivity, or dissatisfaction with appearance. In general terms, evaluations often consider whether these possibilities were explained prior to treatment and how expectations were framed.

The assessment typically depends on the content and clarity of pre-treatment communications, rather than on dissatisfaction alone.

Does returning to another country affect the understanding of dental treatment-related concerns?

Returning abroad after dental treatment does not, by itself, prevent a patient from understanding their general position. Many relevant aspects involve reviewing dental records, treatment plans, consent documents, and pre-treatment communications that remain accessible regardless of location.

As with other aesthetic or medical procedures, general evaluations tend to focus on documentation and prior discussions rather than on physical presence.
