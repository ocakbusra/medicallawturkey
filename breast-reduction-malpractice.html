Understanding Breast Reduction Malpractice in Turkey

Breast reduction surgery, also known as reduction mammoplasty, is a cosmetic procedure performed to reduce the size and weight of overly large breasts, relieve physical discomfort, and achieve a more proportionate breast shape. Many international patients choose Türkiye for this surgery.

Some patients later report outcomes that differ from their expectations or pre-operative discussions.

Commonly Reported Issues After Breast Reduction Procedures

  • Asymmetry between the breasts or nipples.
  • Scarring that is more visible or extensive than expected.
  • Loss of nipple or breast sensation.
  • Breasts appearing too small, too flat, or uneven in shape.
  • Persistent pain or tightness in the chest area.
  • Difficulty with breastfeeding after surgery when this was important to the patient.
  • Results that look significantly different from the desired outcome shown before surgery.
  • Need for revision or corrective surgery.

These are general observations based on patient experiences shared worldwide. Not every unsatisfactory aesthetic result raises legal questions. In cosmetic procedures, the expected outcome and the quality of patient information are often important considerations under Turkish law.

Legal Framework for Aesthetic Procedures

Under Turkish law, aesthetic procedures such as breast reduction are generally evaluated within the scope of contractual obligations (eser sozlesmesi). When concerns arise, the focus is usually on whether the patient was adequately informed about possible results, risks and limitations, and whether the achieved outcome aligns with the discussed expectations.

The Importance of Informed Consent

In cosmetic surgery, the duty to inform the patient is broader than in therapeutic treatments. Before breast reduction, patients are expected to receive clear information regarding:

  • The anticipated size, shape and position of the breasts.
  • Possible scarring patterns and their long-term appearance.
  • Risks of changes in nipple sensation or breastfeeding ability.
  • Realistic expectations and possible need for future revision.
  • Available alternatives.

Information for International Patients

Many patients return to their home countries shortly after the procedure. Foreign patients generally have similar rights to Turkish citizens in matters related to medical liability. Initial steps in understanding a situation can often be handled remotely through proper documentation and communication.

Commonly Considered Steps

  • Collecting medical records, before-and-after photographs, consent forms and all correspondence with the clinic, including WhatsApp messages.
  • Obtaining an independent medical opinion where necessary.
  • Reviewing the general legal framework that may apply under Turkish law.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions Often Raised by Patients After Breast Reduction Procedures

General informational answers based on common patient concerns.

How closely should the final breast reduction result align with what was discussed before surgery?

In breast reduction procedures, pre-operative discussions often address the intended reduction in size and weight, breast shape, nipple position, and overall proportionality with the body. When these aspects are explained or illustrated prior to surgery, the final result is generally considered in relation to those discussions.

From a general legal perspective, attention is often given to whether the achieved outcome reasonably corresponds with what was described beforehand, taking into account anatomical factors and the inherent limitations of reduction procedures.

What level of information is expected regarding scarring and sensory changes?

Breast reduction surgery involves tissue removal and incisions, which may affect scar appearance and, in some cases, sensation in the breast or nipple area. In cosmetic procedures, patients are generally expected to receive clear and detailed information regarding:

  • The location, extent, and visibility of scars.
  • Possible changes in nipple or breast sensation.
  • Healing timelines and variability between individuals.
  • Limitations in achieving complete symmetry.

General considerations often focus on whether these aspects were adequately explained before surgery, allowing patients to form realistic expectations.

How are expectations related to physical relief and aesthetic outcome evaluated?

Breast reduction procedures may be performed to address both physical discomfort, such as neck, shoulder, or back strain, and aesthetic concerns. In general terms, evaluations often consider whether the anticipated degree of relief and the expected aesthetic changes were clearly communicated prior to surgery.

The assessment typically depends on the content of pre-operative communications and whether the patient was informed about factors that could influence both functional and aesthetic outcomes.

Does returning to another country affect the understanding of breast reduction-related concerns?

Returning abroad after a breast reduction procedure does not, by itself, prevent a patient from understanding their general position. Many relevant aspects involve reviewing medical records, consent documents, and pre-operative communications that remain accessible regardless of location.

As with other cosmetic procedures, general evaluations tend to focus on documentation and prior discussions rather than on physical presence.
