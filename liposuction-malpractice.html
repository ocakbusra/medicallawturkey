Understanding Liposuction Malpractice in Turkey

Turkey has become a global destination for health tourism. While many operations are successful, negligence can lead to severe physical, emotional, and financial distress. Liposuction Malpractice claims arise when a physician or clinic fails to adhere to the accepted standard of care, leading to an undesired and damaging outcome.

Common Signs of Medical Negligence

  • Lack of proper informed consent regarding specific risks of Liposuction Malpractice.
  • Unexpected anatomical deformities or functional difficulties.
  • Severe infections or complications ignored by the clinic.
  • False promises and misleading before/after simulations.
  • Being operated on by unqualified personnel or in unlicensed facilities.

Your Legal Options under Turkish Law

Under Turkish jurisprudence, specifically "Contract for Work" (Eser Sözleşmesi) regulations, cosmetic and elective procedures place a heavy burden of proof on the physician to deliver the promised result. Our experienced medical malpractice attorneys will evaluate your medical records, coordinate with independent medical experts, and represent you in Turkish courts to secure the compensation you deserve.

Frequently Asked Questions

Liposuction Malpractice Legal FAQ

Vital information regarding your Liposuction Malpractice case.

What constitutes Liposuction Malpractice under Turkish law?

It includes any negligence, lack of informed consent, or deviation from accepted medical standards during your procedure that resulted in harm, deformity, or complications.

Can I file a claim for Liposuction Malpractice from abroad?

Yes. With a specific Power of Attorney (Vekaletname) granted at your local Turkish consulate, our specialized lawyers can initiate and manage your case in Turkey without you needing to travel.

What compensation can I recover?

Compensation can cover the costs of the original surgery, necessary corrective or revision surgeries, lost wages due to time off work, and moral damages for pain, suffering, and emotional distress.
